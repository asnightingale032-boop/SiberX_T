A Message to SiberX Riders About Today's Incident

We know trust in public transit depends on more than trains running on time. Here's what happened, what we're doing about it, and how to check if you're affected.

What happened

Earlier today, attackers gained access to a shared service account used by our digital signage, PA, and mobile app systems. This allowed false messages to appear on some station screens, PA systems, and in the app. Our security team detected the activity, isolated the affected systems, and began restoring service from verified, trusted infrastructure.

What we're doing

  • Revoked and rotated all exposed credentials
  • Rebuilt affected systems from a trusted, verified environment
  • Engaged an independent forensic review, including national cybersecurity authorities
  • Reviewing vendor account access policies across the network

Frequently asked questions

Was my payment information stolen?

Our investigation to date shows no evidence that stored payment data was accessed. This page will be updated if that changes.

Is it safe to use the app and signage now?

Affected systems have been isolated and rebuilt from a trusted environment. We recommend updating the app to the latest version.

Why did the signage tell me to evacuate?

That message was not issued by SiberX — it was pushed by the attacker through a compromised system. There was no actual safety risk to the network.
